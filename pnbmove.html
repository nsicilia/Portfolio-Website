Project Summary

Pnbmove is an app that saves time and energy for users who simply want to move something too large to fit into their car if they have on at all. It removes the hassle of moving large pieces of furniture using peer-to-peer ride sharing technology and a seamless easy to use interface. My role in creating this app was to research, design, test, and build all systems and UIs related to the project. I collaborated with a UX tester and an advisor in the process of building this project.

Surveys

Once I determined the basic functions and utility of the app. I created and distributed a survey to 108 people to gauge the level of interest in the potential service.

It was also important to collect demographic information on the participants to further refine the app. The survey and statistics gathered from the results where both created using Qualtrics.

Wireframes

Using a pencil and paper, I started creating the general structure of the app and the layout of the screens. I created low-fidelity prototypes to rapidly iterate through designs.

Prototypes

Using Sketch and Axure, I created clickable prototypes to simulate the interaction between the UI screens. These were used to test potential user flows and interaction components.

User Testing

People can often interact with tools in very unexpected ways. To better understand the way people use the app's interface I conducted 10 user tests. The users were asked to complete a series of tasks with the end goal of moving an item they would like to bring home with them. Through user-testing, I realized the way in which users describe the size of the object was far too complicated.

Conclusion

During the user research process, I was genuinely surprised to see how many users either ignored or input random numbers into the measurements section of the size screen. It turned out that many people did not want to deal with precise measurements and as such, I changed the input options to Sml/Med/Lrg with icons to indicate relative sizes.


We found that our overall system usability score (SUS) was 82. The average SUS score is 68.

While the whole project was a huge learning experience, I especially loved iterating on designs and creating the brand guide. Creating clean designs and a consistent style that users would enjoy and understand was incredibly rewarding.
